Describe the effects of changing the spring constant ks; how does the cloth behave from start to rest with a very low ks? A high ks?

With a very high ks value, the cloth is very static. It doesn’t bounce or react as much to any of the forces enacted upon it. In contrast, low values of ks had a “bouncier” effect. That is, the cloth reacted to any force upon it much more drastically than with lower values. This makes sense since ks controls the “springy-ness” of the cloth. The differences were noticeable at the “peak” of the drop of the cloth in pinned4

Density affects how heavy the cloth is. This is most noticeable on pinned 4, where we observed a correlation between how low the cloth sunk and the density value. With higher density values, the cloth sunk lower and was more concave. Lower density values, the cloth looked less concave.

What about for damping?

Damping controls how reactant the cloth is. High values, the cloth doesn’t even bounce, and at low density values the cloth bounces around a lot. Explain in your own words what is a shader program and how vertex and fragment shaders work together to create lighting and material effects

A shader program is program designed to render images on screen. It does this using vertex and fragment shaders. Vertex shaders control the position of vertices, converting 3D coordinates into 2D coordinates to display on screen. Fragment shaders control what the image looks like. The vertex shader calculates the position, scaling, and lighting calculations based on the vertex, then passes this information to the fragment shader. The fragment shader would then calculate the color, light intensity or reflection intensity per pixel.

Bumping mapping is more flat compared to displacement mapping. With displacement mapping, the fine lines along the sphere pop out, giving it a real 3D effect. Whereas bump mapping feels more like the illusion of a detailed 3D surface, but in reality the its still “2D” because its just the texture and bump mapping effect giving off the impression of a 3D line.

Between -o 16 -a 16 and -a 128 -o 128, there wasn’t much of a difference for bump mapping. The lines might be finer but its such a small difference that its very hard to notice. For displacement, the difference is much more clear. At the values for 128, the bumps were small and matched the texture so that ball looked like it had physical scales on it. For displace on the -a 16 and -o 16, the displacement looked a lot less detail, as in it lost a lot of its complex geometry and just looks like random spikes around the sphere. In general, the run with -a 128 and -o 128 made the sphere a lot more round, whereas the former had some sharpe edges along the sphere.
